Type: Cover / First EMI Session

Year: 1962

Writers: Consuelo Velázquez

First Recorded By: The Beatles (EMI Studio 2, London)

Purpose of Session: To record the band’s first single for EMI

Recording Session

  • Date: 6 June 1962
  • Studio: EMI, Abbey Road, Studio Two
  • Producer: George Martin
  • Personnel:
    • John Lennon – lead vocals, rhythm guitar
    • Paul McCartney – bass, backing vocals
    • George Harrison – lead guitar, backing vocals
    • Ringo Starr – drums
  • Takes: multiple takes recorded; exact take info in Lewisohn

Notes & Anecdotes

This session was aimed at tracking their first EMI single, which would eventually be “Love Me Do.” Though “Bésame Mucho” was never released as a single, it marks the very first time the Beatles entered EMI studios under George Martin’s supervision.

Technical Details

Mono recording, 4-track setup at Abbey Road Studio Two. Tape speed, mic setup, and other technical details can be referenced from Hammack's manuals.
