Type: Cover / First EMI Session

Year: 1962

Writers: Consuelo Velázquez

First Recorded By: The Beatles (EMI Studio 2, London)

Purpose of Session: To record the band’s first single for EMI

Recording Session

Date 6 June 1962
Studio EMI, Abbey Road, Studio Two
Producer George Martin
Personnel
  • John Lennon – lead vocals, rhythm guitar
  • Paul McCartney – bass, backing vocals
  • George Harrison – lead guitar, backing vocals
  • Ringo Starr – drums
Takes Multiple takes recorded; exact take info in Lewisohn

Notes & Anecdotes

This session was aimed at tracking their first EMI single, which would eventually be “Love Me Do.” Though “Bésame Mucho” was never released as a single, it marks the very first time the Beatles entered EMI studios under George Martin’s supervision. The band’s enthusiasm and energy during this session foreshadowed their rapid rise to fame.

Technical Details

Mono recording on a 4-track setup at Abbey Road Studio Two. Tape speed, mic placement, and additional recording techniques are documented in Hammack’s manuals. This session represents the beginning of the Beatles’ collaboration with George Martin as producer.
